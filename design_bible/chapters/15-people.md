# Chapter 15 — People

# Purpose

The People domain is the canonical representation of every person that matters to the user.

People are not merely contacts. They are relationships that connect the entire Perch ecosystem.

---

# Primary Question

**Who matters, what matters about them, and what should I know right now?**

---

# Design Intent

The People page should help users understand their relationships, commitments, history, and future interactions without becoming an address book.

---

# Read Mode

Shows what matters now:
- Upcoming birthdays
- Recent interactions
- Shared goals/projects
- Health or care reminders
- Relationship timeline

---

# Explore Mode

Allows exploration by:
- Family
- Friends
- Coworkers
- Medical providers
- Vendors
- Pets (optional relationship type)
- Organizations

---

# Core Information

Each person may contain:
- Identity
- Relationship
- Contact methods
- Important dates
- Linked properties
- Linked projects
- Linked goals
- Linked obligations
- Notes
- Documents
- Interaction history

---

# Connected Engines

- Truth Engine
- Priority Engine
- Recommendation Engine
- Voice Engine

---

# Connected Domains

- Today
- Calendar
- Money
- Goals
- Projects
- Brain
- Home
- Knowledge
- Search

---

# AI Responsibilities

The AI should:
- Surface meaningful reminders.
- Detect stale relationship information.
- Connect people to relevant context.
- Never invent facts about relationships.

---

# What Does NOT Belong

- Social media feed
- Chat application
- CRM complexity
- Public profile scraping

---

# Future Expansion

- Household management
- Shared planning
- Caregiver mode
- Emergency planning
- Family knowledge graph

---

# Success Criteria

A user can immediately understand:
- Who matters.
- What is happening with them.
- What commitments exist.
- What needs attention.
- Why Perch surfaced this information.
